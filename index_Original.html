Why Records Matter

Wildlife data doesn't record itself.
Someone has to.

Every planning decision, every conservation project, every Species Action Plan in Kent depends on accurate, validated biological records. Without an organisation like KMBRC aggregating and verifying this data, critical habitats go unprotected and developers fill the gap with silence. That's why what you contribute — whether a sighting or a survey — genuinely matters.

Our Work

Four ways KMBRC
serves Kent's environment

Data Collection & Validation

We collect wildlife records from public contributors, ecological surveyors, and specialist recording groups — then rigorously validate each one. Over 9 million validated records and counting, built up over 35 years.

Planning Support

Our data informs hundreds of planning applications each year, ensuring development decisions are grounded in verified ecological evidence.

Education & Research

From school programmes to university research partnerships, we open our data to the next generation of ecologists and conservationists.

Small Grants Scheme

We fund local recording groups and conservation projects through the KMBRC Small Grants Scheme. Applications open annually.
